Discover Sula Valley!

Our Culture

Sula Valley is a city with a rich cultural heritage. It is a city of cheerful and jovial people, a city full of contrasts, flavors, colors and much love for culture. It is a city full of contrasts, flavors, colors and a lot of love for culture, from our roots to the updated contrasts of pop culture. It is a bilingual city where a large part of its population is fluent in English as well as Spanish.
In Sula Valley, the cultural agenda is broad and a very important part of its organization falls on the shoulders of the Municipality, which organizes important events in favor of the cultural development of the city. One of the most important events is the 'Feria Juniana' is the patron saint fair of Sula Valley and is held in honor of its patron saint, 'San Pedro'. This fair began to be held in 1846, to become today, one of the best and largest events in Honduras.

Our music

Marimba Usula:

It is a sampedrana marimba that fights to keep alive the essence of the chapina and catracha in Honduras. They have toured several cities in the USA. They have been on stage with several Mexican artists. They traveled to South Korea. The group disappeared because of Hurricane Fifi in 1974 and in 1979 the Marimba Usula Municipal resurfaced.

Our Cultural Sites of Interest

Museum of Anthropology and History

The museum opened its doors to the public on January 25, 1994; easily accessible, located on 3rd Avenue between 3rd and 4th Street N.W. Its doors are open to visitors Monday through Saturday from 9:00 AM to 4:00 PM and Sundays from 9:00 AM to 3:00 PM. The museum offers the public part of the history beginning with the arrival of the Spaniards to Honduran territory. The wars and conquests, the consequent cultural and racial mixing, the founding of San Pedro and the banana exploitation. The Museum of Anthropology and History protects the national heritage, educates, disseminates culture, promotes research and reinforces an identity.

Our Education

Sampedrano Cultural Center (CCS)

The Sampedrano Cultural Center, CCS, is a binational institution supported by the U.S. Embassy. It was born in 1955 at the initiative of a group of Hondurans and Americans, as an entity to strengthen the ties of friendship between the two nations.
The CCS is an institution of secondary education and English language learning, promoting art and culture in all its manifestations. Its mission is to positively influence the construction of a better Honduras, through the integral formation of young people who are part of the student community and, likewise, making known the national and foreign artistic values, to strengthen Honduran culture.

Demographic Data

It is the second largest city in the Central American state of Honduras, located in the wide area of the isthmus that connects North and South America.
Located north of Tegucigalpa, capital of the country, Sula Valley occupies an area of 871 Km2 and has a population of 567,713 inhabitants. The Metropolitan Zone of the Sula Valley includes 15 other municipalities in addition to San Pedro, which cover 4,160 km2 and are home to one fifth of the Honduran population (1,600,000 people).
